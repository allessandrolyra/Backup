# Presentation Review & Improvement

Perform comprehensive review of existing presentations and provide structured, actionable improvement recommendations.

## Activation

When the user shares an existing presentation for review, follow this process:

### Step 1 — Understand Review Scope

Determine review focus:

- **Full review** — Complete assessment across all dimensions
- **Narrative review** — Focus on storyline, flow, and messaging
- **Design review** — Focus on visual consistency, layout, and readability
- **Content review** — Focus on accuracy, completeness, and synthesis quality
- **Audience adaptation** — Assess fit for a specific audience
- **Quick fix** — Address a specific problem the user identified

### Step 2 — Multi-Dimensional Analysis

Evaluate across these dimensions:

**1. Message Clarity**
- Is there one clear message per slide?
- Are titles informative (not generic)?
- Can the audience understand each slide in under 10 seconds?

**2. Narrative Structure**
- Does the deck tell a cohesive story?
- Is there a logical flow from beginning to end?
- Are transitions between sections smooth?
- Is there a strong opening and a strong closing?

**3. Content Density**
- Is there too much text on any slide?
- Are bullet points concise (8-12 words)?
- Is data presented as insights rather than raw numbers?
- Are there redundant slides that can be merged?

**4. Visual Consistency**
- Are fonts, colors, and styles consistent?
- Are layouts aligned and spaced properly?
- Is the visual hierarchy clear (title > subtitle > body > footnote)?
- Is there appropriate contrast between text and background?

**5. Typography & Readability**
- Are font sizes adequate for projection?
- Is there readable contrast?
- Are fonts appropriate for corporate context?
- Is there excessive use of uppercase, italic, or underline?

**6. Audience Adequacy**
- Is the language appropriate for the target audience?
- Is the level of detail right?
- Are technical terms explained when needed?
- Does the deck match audience expectations?

**7. Branding Adherence**
- Does the deck follow the organization's visual identity?
- Are logo, colors, and fonts consistent with brand guidelines?
- Are layouts aligned with corporate template standards?

**8. Chart & Data Usage**
- Are charts the right type for the data?
- Are charts labeled and readable?
- Do chart headlines state the insight, not just the topic?
- Are tables simplified for presentation context?

### Step 3 — Structured Report

Deliver the review in this format:

```
## Review Summary

### Strengths
- [What works well — be specific]

### Critical Issues (Fix First)
1. [Issue] — [Impact] — [Recommended Fix]
2. [Issue] — [Impact] — [Recommended Fix]

### Priority Improvements
1. [Improvement] — [Expected Impact] — [How to Implement]
2. [Improvement] — [Expected Impact] — [How to Implement]

### Secondary Improvements
- [Nice-to-have improvement 1]
- [Nice-to-have improvement 2]

### Final Recommendation
[Overall assessment and recommended next steps]
```

### Step 4 — Offer Remediation

After the review, offer to:

1. **Restructure** the deck narrative
2. **Rewrite** specific slides with improved content
3. **Redesign** layout recommendations for problem slides
4. **Generate** an improved version of the full deck
5. **Create** speaker notes for the improved version

## Severity Classification

| Level | Description | Action |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| Critical | Undermines the presentation's objective | Must fix before presenting |
| High | Significantly reduces impact | Should fix |
| Medium | Noticeable quality gap | Recommended fix |
| Low | Polish-level improvement | Optional enhancement |
