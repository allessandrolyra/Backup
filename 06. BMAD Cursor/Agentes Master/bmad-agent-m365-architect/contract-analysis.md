# Contract Analysis

## Purpose

Explain Microsoft licensing contracts, channels, billing models and commercial terms to support informed purchase, renewal and optimization decisions.

## Process

1. **Identify the contract context**:
   - Which channel? (CSP, EA, Direct)
   - Which contract type? (MCA, EA, EAS, SCE, MPSA)
   - New purchase, renewal, or mid-term change?
   - Organization size and segment (SMB, Enterprise, Government, Education)
   - Country/region (impacts availability and terms)

2. **Explain the contract structure**:

   ### CSP (Cloud Solution Provider)
   - Partner-managed billing and support
   - NCE (New Commerce Experience) terms
   - Monthly vs. annual vs. triennial commitments
   - Cancellation policies and windows
   - Seat adjustment rules
   - Price protection (when applicable)
   - Partner margin and value-added services

   ### EA (Enterprise Agreement)
   - Minimum commitment (500+ users typically)
   - 3-year term with annual true-up
   - Price lock for the term
   - Product selection flexibility
   - Step-up licensing
   - Broad and narrow use rights

   ### EAS (Enterprise Agreement Subscription)
   - Subscription-based EA
   - True-down capability at anniversary
   - Pay only for what you use

   ### SCE (Server and Cloud Enrollment)
   - On-premises + cloud hybrid licensing
   - Software Assurance benefits
   - Azure Hybrid Benefit alignment

   ### MCA (Microsoft Customer Agreement)
   - Direct relationship with Microsoft
   - Self-service management in admin center
   - Required for CSP customers
   - Acceptance tracking

   ### MPSA (Microsoft Products and Services Agreement)
   - Transactional purchasing
   - Volume discounts based on pools
   - No minimum commitment
   - Flexibility for varied purchases

3. **Billing analysis**:
   - Payment frequency options
   - Currency and regional pricing
   - Pro-rating rules
   - Credit/refund policies
   - Invoice structure
   - Cost center allocation

4. **Decision support**:
   - Contract comparison for the user's scenario
   - Advantages and disadvantages of each option
   - Migration path from current to recommended contract
   - Timing considerations (renewal windows, fiscal year alignment)
   - Risk factors (commitment lock-in, cancellation penalties)

5. **Deliver** with:
   - Contract recommendation with justification
   - Timeline for action
   - Stakeholders to involve (procurement, legal, Microsoft, partner)
   - Official documentation references

## Key Rules

- Always differentiate: channel ≠ contract ≠ commercial model ≠ enrollment
- Never issue definitive commercial opinions without flagging need for validation
- Inform when pricing/terms vary by region or segment
- Alert when legal or procurement review is recommended
- Cite Product Terms as primary authority for usage rights
- NCE rules change frequently — always flag validation date
